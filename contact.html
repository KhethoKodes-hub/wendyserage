Our Services

Empowering Communities Through Sustainable Farming

01

Youth Mentorship

One-on-one mentorship for unemployed young people. We train and guide them to start their own farms and agro-businesses, just like our three successful mentees who now run independent farms.

02

Practical Training

Monthly hands-on training sessions on our farm. We welcome farmers and youth from all nine provinces of South Africa, Botswana, and Zimbabwe. At least five local people trained for free each month.

03

Student Placements

We host 20+ students and graduates from agricultural colleges like Tompi Seleka, Letaba College, and Vhembe TVET. Real-world experience that prepares them for successful farming careers.

04

Cabbage Production

Premium quality fresh cabbages grown sustainably. We also donate fresh produce to local crèches: Kids Kidos, Noko Crèche, and Mami's Crèche, supporting child nutrition in our community.

05

Agro-Business Workshops

Short, focused training sessions that produce immediate results. After our Regen College workshop, participants started agro-processing businesses and home gardens within two weeks.

06

Community Food Donations

Regular fresh cabbage donations to three local crèches: Kids Kidos, Noko Crèche, and Mami's Crèche. Ensuring vulnerable children receive healthy, nutritious meals.

07

Rural Economic Growth

Our training sessions bring visitors who support local guesthouses, shops, and transport services. We are creating a ripple effect of economic opportunity in our village.

08

Cross-Border Training

Farmers and aspiring agripreneurs from Botswana and Zimbabwe attend our monthly sessions. We are building regional partnerships to share knowledge and best practices across borders.

Our Story

From collecting cans to leading a farming revolution

Wendy Moshakga started Serage Holdings in 2021 with no land, no money, and no training. Today, her farm trains students from multiple agricultural colleges, mentors unemployed youth, and supports local businesses — all while growing premium cabbages.
